Portfolio

TiPay. Easy as Tea.

Manage your banking online and make payments from TiPay - the easy way!

TiPay is a fully responsive, online banking application prototype which allows customers to manage their money by viewing their account details and making payments to other accounts. The web application includes a functional mock-up of register, login, account, payment and support pages.

All forms are validated and provide live visual feedback to the user via the colour of the button or forms; grey for incomplete, red for invalid and green for valid. The user can choose to add payees to their list via their mobile or bank account details and send money to either their other accounts or any payees from the list. Actions made by the user such as adding new payees and performing transactions will update information by temporarily saving data to the browser's session storage. The user will be able to view updated new payees, account balances and recent transactions until browser close.

The logo was designed to represent the core values of the 'brand' which were reflected onto the design.

  1. 'Ti' for TiPay,
  2. 'Teacup' is the brand symbol and slogan,
  3. 'Human' for user friendlyness,
  4. 'Coin' above the teacup,
  5. and 'Green' for money.

Country Explorer

Search any country in the world to discover facts and a live snapshot of the capital city!

Country Explorer utilises multiple APIs to gather information about countries and its respective capital city. REST countries, OpenWeatherMap and a variety of Google APIs such as Maps, Translate, Time Zone and Custom Image Search were used. Users can search countries by full name to display results immediately or keywords to generate a list of countries to choose from. Countries with multiple official languages will have a feature to translate the greeting text to the available languages.

Calorie Calculator

Find your recommended daily calorie intake with an animated multi-step form!

Calorie Calculator implements a step-by-step approach to gather information from the user such as gender, age, weight and height to estimate daily recommended calorie intake based on the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation which calculates a person’s basal metabolic rate (BMR). Each type of input has been intuitively styled and animated to respond to the user’s interactions and indicate validation. The user can progress and focus on the next input by passing validation and then pressing enter or touching the arrow for touch screen devices. On the last page, the user’s profile and the results will be displayed.
